The RATSL dataset is from a rat nutrition study by Crowder and Hand (1990), in which three groups of rats were put on dierent diets, and each animals body weight (grams) was recorded repeatedly (approximately weekly, except in week seven when two recordings were taken) over a 9-week period.

The BPRSL dataset is from Davis (2002). In the experiment 40 male subjects were randomly assigned to one of two treatment groups and each subject was rated on the brief psychiatric rating scale (BPRS) measured before treatment be- gan (week 0) and then at weekly intervals for eight weeks. The BPRS assesses the level of 18 symptom constructs such as hostility, suspiciousness, halluci- nations and grandiosity; each of these is rated from one (not present) to seven (extremely severe). The scale is used to evaluate patients suspected of having schizophrenia.

Now we plot a box plot of the data. Here we also filter out the baseline observations/measurements. Baseline measurements of the outcome variable in a longitudinal study are often correlated with the chosen summary measure and using such measures in the analysis can often lead to substantial gains in precision when used appropriately as a covariate in an analysis of covariance.

# Create a summary data by Group and ID with mean as the summary variable (ignoring baseline i.e. Time 0).
